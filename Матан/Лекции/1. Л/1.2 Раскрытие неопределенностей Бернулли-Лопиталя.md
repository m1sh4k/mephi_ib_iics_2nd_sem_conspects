
## Теорема 

$f(x),g(x)$ дифф на $(a,b]$

Если 
1. $\lim\limits_{ x \to a^{+} }f(x)=\lim\limits_{ x \to a^{+} }g(x)=0\ или\ \infty$
2. $\exists \lim\limits_{ x \to a^{+} } \dfrac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}=A$

То
$\exists \lim\limits_{ x \to a^{+} } \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}=A$

По теореме конечных приращений Коши: $\displaystyle\exists \xi \in (x;x_{0}): \frac{{f(x)-f(x_{0})}}{g(x)-g(x_{0})}=\frac{f'(\xi)}{g'(\xi)}$
$$
\begin{align*}
& f(x)=f(x_{0})+\frac{f'(\xi)}{g'(\xi)}(g(x)-g(x_{0})) \quad\bigg|\div g(x)\\
& \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\frac{f(x_{0})}{g(x)}+\frac{f'(\xi)}{g'(\xi)}-\frac{f'(\xi)g(x_{0})}{g'(\xi)g(x)} \\
& \left| \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}-A \right| \leq \left| \frac{f(x_{0})}{g(x)} \right| +\left| \frac{f'(\xi)}{g'(\xi)}-A \right| +\left| \frac{f'(\xi)}{g'(\xi)}\cdot \frac{g(x_{0})}{g(x)} \right| 
\end{align*}
$$

$$
\begin{align*}
& \forall\varepsilon>0\quad\exists\delta_{\varepsilon}:\forall x_{0} \in (a;a+\delta_{\varepsilon}) \\
& \left| \frac{f'(x_{0})}{g'(x_{0})} \right| <\varepsilon \\ 
& \left| \frac{f'(\xi)}{g'(\xi)}-A \right|<\varepsilon  \\
& \lim\limits_{ x \to x_{0} } \frac{g(x_{0})}{g(x)}=0 \\
& \left| \frac{f'(\xi)}{g'(\xi)}\cdot \frac{g(x_{0})}{g(x)} \right| \to0 \\
& \left| \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}-A \right|<2\varepsilon 
\end{align*}
$$

1. Если $a=+\infty \quad t:= \dfrac{1}{x}$
   $\lim\limits_{ x \to +\infty } \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}=\lim\limits_{ t \to +0 } \dfrac{f\left( \frac{1}{t} \right)}{g\left( \frac{1}{t} \right)}=\lim\limits_{ t \to 0^{+} } \dfrac{f'\left( \frac{1}{t} \right)\left( -\frac{1}{t^{2}} \right)}{g\left( \frac{1}{t}\left( -\frac{1}{t^{2}} \right) \right)}$
